# Finger Rehabilitation Exercises

**Target:** Joint pain between pinky and ring finger (left hand)
**Status:** Active rehabilitation needed
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-14

## Current Injury Context

**Injury:** Fall-related trauma to joint area between left pinky and ring finger
**Goals:** 
- Reduce pain and inflammation
- Restore range of motion
- Prevent stiffness
- Return to full hand function

## Exercise Protocol

### Phase 1: Initial Healing (Days 1-3)

**Focus:** Pain management and gentle movement

#### 1. Gentle Range of Motion
- **Frequency:** 3-4 times daily
- **Duration:** 5-10 repetitions each
- **Instructions:**
  - Gently flex and extend pinky finger
  - Stop at first sign of pain
  - Hold end positions for 2-3 seconds

#### 2. Adjacent Finger Movement
- **Frequency:** 3 times daily  
- **Instructions:**
  - Move ring finger independently
  - Gentle finger spreading (abduction)
  - Avoid forcing movement

### Phase 2: Progressive Mobilization (Days 4-7)

**Focus:** Increase range of motion and reduce stiffness

#### 3. Finger Flexion/Extension
- **Frequency:** 4-5 times daily
- **Sets:** 2-3 sets of 10-15 repetitions
- **Instructions:**
  - Make gentle fist, then extend fingers
  - Focus on smooth, controlled movement
  - Gradually increase range

#### 4. Finger Spreading
- **Frequency:** 3-4 times daily
- **Sets:** 2 sets of 10 repetitions
- **Instructions:**
  - Spread fingers apart gently
  - Hold for 3-5 seconds
  - Focus on pinky-ring finger separation

#### 5. Tendon Glides
- **Frequency:** 3 times daily
- **Sets:** 1-2 sets of 5-8 repetitions
- **Positions:**
  1. Straight fingers
  2. Hook fist (bend at knuckles)
  3. Full fist
  4. Straight fist (fingertips to palm base)

### Phase 3: Strengthening (Week 2+)

**Focus:** Build strength and endurance

#### 6. Resistance Exercises
- **Frequency:** Daily
- **Equipment:** Rubber band or therapy putty
- **Instructions:**
  - Finger extension against resistance
  - Pinch grip strengthening
  - Start light, progress gradually

#### 7. Functional Activities
- **Frequency:** Throughout day
- **Activities:**
  - Writing/typing with focus on pinky use
  - Picking up small objects
  - Buttoning clothes
  - Playing piano/keyboard motions

## Warning Signs - Stop Exercises If:

- Sharp, severe pain
- Sudden swelling or redness
- Numbness or tingling
- Loss of movement
- No improvement after 1 week

## Progress Monitoring

### Daily Assessments
- [ ] Pain level (1-10 scale)
- [ ] Range of motion (compare to right hand)
- [ ] Swelling or discoloration
- [ ] Functional ability

### Weekly Goals
- **Week 1:** Pain reduction, basic movement
- **Week 2:** Improved range of motion
- **Week 3:** Near-normal function
- **Week 4:** Return to full activities

## Professional Consultation

**Consider seeing healthcare provider if:**
- No improvement in 3-5 days
- Worsening symptoms
- Suspected fracture
- Severe functional limitation

**Potential Providers:**
- Primary care physician
- Hand therapist
- Orthopedic specialist
- Physical therapist

## Heat/Cold Therapy

### Ice (First 48-72 hours)
- **Duration:** 15-20 minutes
- **Frequency:** 3-4 times daily
- **Method:** Ice pack wrapped in towel

### Heat (After initial inflammation)
- **Duration:** 15-20 minutes
- **Frequency:** Before exercises
- **Method:** Warm compress or warm water soak

## Activity Modifications

### Temporary Restrictions
- Heavy gripping activities
- Forceful pinching
- Sports requiring hand impact
- Repetitive typing/writing (limit duration)

### Ergonomic Considerations
- Use larger grip tools when possible
- Take frequent breaks from hand activities
- Maintain good wrist/hand positioning

---

*This rehabilitation protocol should be adapted based on pain levels and healing progress. Consult healthcare providers for persistent or worsening symptoms.*
