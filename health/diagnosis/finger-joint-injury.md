# Finger Joint Injury - Left Hand

**Diagnosis:** Traumatic injury to joint area between pinky and ring finger
**Status:** ACTIVE - Affects daily functioning
**Onset:** 2026-03-14 (following fall)
**Impact Level:** Moderate - affects hand function, typing, grip activities

## Clinical Summary

**Primary Diagnosis:** Post-traumatic joint pain and possible soft tissue injury
- **Location:** Left hand, joint area between 4th and 5th digits
- **Mechanism:** Fall-related trauma
- **Current Symptoms:** Joint pain, possible stiffness
- **Functional Impact:** Reduced grip strength, typing limitations, hand function compromise

## Daily Functional Impact

### Work/Computer Activities
- **Typing limitations:** Requires frequent breaks, modified positioning
- **Grip activities:** Reduced strength for opening jars, carrying objects
- **Fine motor tasks:** Difficulty with small objects, precise movements
- **Ergonomic needs:** Modified hand positioning, ergonomic support required

### Activities of Daily Living
- **Writing:** May require larger grip tools, frequent breaks
- **Driving:** Monitor grip strength on steering wheel
- **Sports/Exercise:** Avoid impact activities, modify exercise routines
- **Household tasks:** Caution with gripping, lifting, carrying

## Current Treatment Plan

### Active Interventions
- **Rehabilitation exercises:** 3-4 times daily (see rehabilitation protocol)
- **Activity modifications:** Ergonomic adjustments, frequent breaks
- **Pain management:** As needed with OTC medications
- **Progress monitoring:** Daily pain assessment, weekly function evaluation

### Treatment Goals
- **Short-term (1-2 weeks):** Pain reduction, inflammation control
- **Medium-term (2-4 weeks):** Restore range of motion
- **Long-term (4-6 weeks):** Return to full hand function

2. Adjacent finger movement (independent ring finger movement)

## Warning Signs for Medical Attention

**Immediate consultation needed if:**
- Sharp, severe pain (8+ on scale) that doesn't improve with rest
- Sudden loss of movement or sensation
- Signs of infection (redness, warmth, swelling, streaking)
- Numbness or tingling that persists

**Consider medical evaluation if:**
- No improvement in pain after 1 week
- Decreased range of motion despite exercises
- Significant functional limitations affecting work/daily activities
- Persistent pain level 6+ after 3-4 days
